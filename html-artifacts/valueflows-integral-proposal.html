Integral × ValueFlows · A proposal for the working groups

Keep our own books,
or use a shared ledger?

A contributor has sketched how Integral's data could map onto ValueFlows — a twenty-year-old, widely-used vocabulary for cooperative and commons economies. This page explains, in plain terms, what that mapping gets right, where it stops, and what we'd have to decide before adopting it. Every claim is tied back to the white paper and Dev Guide — and to the ValueFlows spec itself.

// What's being proposed, and why it's worth a look

Every economy is, underneath, a set of books

Who did what work. What was made. What materials were used. Who can access what, and at what cost. Strip away the words and an economy is a careful, permanent record of those flows. Integral is no different — its five systems exist to keep exactly those books honestly.

So far we've been designing our own bookkeeping from scratch. ValueFlows (VF) offers another path: a shared, open vocabulary for describing economic flows that a whole family of cooperative and commons projects already speaks. It's built on a well-worn accounting theory called REA — Resources, Events, Agents — and it's deliberately neutral: it can describe a worker co-op or a corner shop equally well.

The question this proposal puts to the group is simple: do we write our own books, or adopt the shared language — carefully? Helpfully, we already have ValueFlows expertise in the room. Lynn Foster, who maintains ValueFlows, and Leanne, who builds ValueFlows accounting models, are both on the ITC workstream.

"Integral replaces speculative price signals with real biophysical metrics, transparent design information, and cybernetic feedback… informed by energy cost, material availability, labor capacity, ecological impact, and actual need — not by the willingness or ability to pay."Technical White Paper · §4.5 True Economic Calculation

That sentence is the test ValueFlows has to pass. VF is happy to record prices. Integral is built to do without them. Keep that tension in mind — it's the thread running through everything below.

📖 What ValueFlows actually is, in its own words +

"The ValueFlows core is based on the REA (Resource, Event, Agent) ontology. To that we have added an IPO (Input-Process-Output) pattern. Agents perform Economic Events that provide Inputs to Processes and take Outputs from Processes and move Economic Resources from one Process to another."

VF organises everything across three layers: a Knowledge level (classifications, recipes, rules), a Plan level (offers, requests, schedules, promises), and an Observation level (what really happened).

// The headline finding

It fits three of our five systems — and can't touch the other two

ValueFlows describes flows of resources. That makes it a near-perfect fit for the three systems whose job is to move materials, labour, and credits around — and structurally unable to express the two systems that decide and self-correct. That isn't a flaw. It's a clean boundary, and a useful one to name.

// Why the fit is genuine, not forced

Our pipeline already runs down VF's three layers

This is the part that makes the mapping more than a coincidence. ValueFlows stacks everything in three layers, and Integral's value loop walks straight down them:

📖 Why "what happened" must be append-only — and why VF agrees (Dev Guide) +

"Every production plan, task assignment, labor event, material consumption, QA record, and distribution event is recorded in an append-only ledger. The ledger is the source of truth for ITC valuation and FRS monitoring… No record is ever deleted or modified — only appended to with correction events that reference the original."

Dev Guide · COS Module 9 (ledger) · view on GitHub ↗

VF reaches the same rule from the accounting side: in VF, "inventory cannot be changed without an economic event," and an event can only ever correct another — never erase it. Two traditions, one principle. DEV GUIDE VALUEFLOWS

// The three mappings, in plain terms

Where each system lands

Each of our three "metabolic" systems lines up with a specific ValueFlows idea. Tap any source box to read the exact white-paper or Dev-Guide passage it rests on.

OAD · OPEN ACCESS DESIGN→ VF Recipe + ResourceSpecification

A certified design becomes a reusable "recipe"

A CertifiedDesign maps to a VF Recipe — a template of process steps and material inputs. VF draws a line we currently blur: the recipe (how to make the thing) is separate from the design artefact itself (the CAD file), which is just another resource with version history. That split is arguably cleaner than ours, and it matches how we already think about design lineage.

One thing VF won't do for us: enforce that production may only use a certified design. That gate is an Integral rule, and it has to live in our layer above VF.

📖 The certification gate is non-negotiable (Dev Guide · OAD Module 9) +

"Certification is the gate between design and production, and it must exist in the minimal build… A design is certified when it has passed ecological assessment, has a complete and coherent bill of materials, and has been reviewed and approved by the certification panel… Uncertified designs cannot enter production. This constraint is enforced architecturally."

Dev Guide · OAD Module 9 · view on GitHub ↗
📖 How VF thinks of recipes vs. designs +

"In VF, recipes are thought of more like templates to generate repeatable plans, rather than designs. Designs might be CAD files, etc., and would show lineage just like other economic resources." Recipes handle the bill of materials and production steps; input flows handle consumables, work, and equipment use.

COS · COOPERATIVE ORGANIZATION→ VF EconomicEvent

Doing the work is recording an "event"

Here VF is at its strongest. Our LaborEvent and MaterialConsumptionEvent collapse into a single VF idea — the EconomicEvent — told apart only by their action: labour is a work event, materials are a consume event, a tool is use, a design reference is cite. Inventory updates itself as a side-effect of events being recorded — you can't quietly change the books, you can only add an event. That is precisely our append-only principle, arrived at independently.

ITC · INTEGRAL TIME CREDITS→ VF EconomicResource + events

A credit account is just another "stock" that can rise and fall

A member's ITCAccount is modelled as an EconomicResource — the same way VF tracks a pile of timber. Earning credit is an event that raises the balance; decay is a scheduled event that lowers it; spending (extinguishment) is an event that consumes it. The catch: the actual weighting of a contribution — skill, difficulty, urgency — is Integral policy that sits on top. VF carries the raw hours; it does not carry our economics. (More on that in the tensions below.)

📖 What credits actually are (White Paper · §5.4 / Transition) +

"Non-transferable time credits prevent accumulation and eliminate the possibility of converting contribution into long-term power." Credits decay over time, "preventing hierarchical stratification," and there is an "extinguishing of credits upon use, preventing accumulation, trade, speculation, or conversion into power."

Technical White Paper · §5.4 ITC & §11 Transition principles
📖 The four ITC properties that can never be deferred (Dev Guide) +

"These four properties — honest recording, embodied valuation, decay, and non-transferability — are the ITC's foundational principles. All four must be present and architecturally enforced from day one. None of them are deferrable."

// The single best idea to take from this

"Credits can't be transferred" stops being a flag, and becomes a rule of grammar

This is the most elegant thing the mapping surfaces. VF's vocabulary already separates two kinds of action: changing a quantity in place (raise, lower, consume) and handing something to someone else (transfer, move, copy). So our hardest ITC promise — that credits are non-transferable and vanish when used — becomes a single, checkable sentence:

Non-transferability, expressed natively

A credit account may only ever be raised (earned), lowered (decayed), or consumed (spent) — and may never be transferred, moved, or copied to another person.

📖 "Credits must not be transferable… even in the prototype" (Dev Guide) +

"That labor contributions are recorded in a way that is transparent, non-transferable, and ecologically aware from the beginning… Credits must not be transferable between participants, even in the prototype."

Dev Guide · Non-deferrable principles · view on GitHub ↗
// What ValueFlows deliberately can't say

Governance and feedback stay ours

Map it to the white paper's village: ValueFlows is the communal ledger, the shared notebook, and the work teams. It says nothing about the open meetings or the household feedback visits — because deciding and self-correcting aren't resource flows. This is exactly the right boundary to draw.

CDS · governance NO VF ANALOG

Weighted consensus, objection mapping, authorising action. VF's closest ideas — Proposal and Agreement — are commercial offer/accept primitives, not collective deliberation. And critically, CDS is what sets the rules ITC then runs on.

FRS · feedback NO VF ANALOG

Diagnostics, fairness monitoring, recommendations. VF describes what happened; it never judges it. FRS reads the VF observation layer as its raw signal — but its conclusions live entirely outside VF.

📖 Why CDS can't live in VF: it sets the policy, ITC just executes it (Dev Guide §4.2) +

"CDS → ITC: The Policy Signal — The contract by which CDS governance decisions become the normative rules that ITC operates within. This includes labor weighting parameters, decay rules, fairness thresholds, access policy modifications, and anti-coercion boundaries. ITC does not set its own policy — CDS does."

Dev Guide §4.2 · view on GitHub ↗
📖 Why FRS can't live in VF: it recommends, it never acts (Dev Guide · FRS) +

"submit_certification_review_request does not halt production. It submits a non-executive recommendation… FRS flags the divergence. It does not pull the trigger. Production continues unless and until CDS ratifies a decision to the contrary… This boundary is what prevents FRS diagnostic authority from becoming a form of algorithmic shadow governance."

// Three things to weigh before we commit

Where the fit needs discipline

None of these are deal-breakers. They're the places where adopting VF carelessly would quietly cost us something. The Dev Guide gives us the exact test to apply.

"If this simplification were never revisited, would the system still be recognizably Integral?"Integral Dev Guide · §1.2
The three tensions
TENSION-01VF is neutral about money — Integral is not+
In plain terms
ValueFlows can describe a market just as happily as a commons. It has a built-in mediumOfExchange flag and can record prices. We'd be adopting a tool that knows how to do the very thing we're built to avoid.
Why it matters
The white paper is unambiguous that price-based coordination is the thing we're replacing. Letting market-shaped features sit dormant in our core is exactly the kind of "approximated-away principle" the Dev Guide warns is a corruption, not a simplification.
Direction on the table
Proposed: adopt VF as a constrained profile — credit resources flagged non-mediumOfExchange, and the forbidden-action rule from above enforced.
Worth weighing: who maintains that constraint list, and how do we keep it from eroding over time?
Source

TENSION-02VF carries the raw hours — not how we value them+
In plain terms
VF records "3 hours of work" cleanly. But turning that into credited value — weighting for skill, difficulty, urgency — is our economics. VF gives us the substrate, not the calculation.
Why it matters
The hardest, most contested part of Integral — how a contribution is weighted — stays Integral-native either way. We should be clear-eyed: VF does not de-risk True Economic Calculation; it just feeds it clean, value-neutral inputs. Happily, that "keep raw and weighted separate" instinct is already in our Dev Guide.
Direction on the table
Proposed: let VF's effortQuantity / resourceQuantity be the raw labour record; keep the weighting as an Integral layer that produces the credited value.
Worth weighing: this is the same raw-vs-weighted boundary as the ITC schema workstream's open question — they should be resolved together.

"The white paper separates raw labor capture (LaborEvent) from weighted contribution recognition (WeightedLaborRecord). This is architecturally significant: the labor event is value-neutral until weighting is applied… the boundary between raw capture and weighted output must be preserved in the field structure."

TENSION-03Federation: VF can express it, but won't enforce it+
In plain terms
We tag every record with its node from day one. VF handles multiple communities through its Agent concept. But our rule that credits are recognised, never converted across nodes — the home node re-mints fresh credit rather than receiving any — is something VF can represent but won't police.
Why it matters
"Recognition without conversion" is how cross-node reciprocity avoids becoming a back-door currency. If nothing enforces it, the constraint is only as good as everyone's good behaviour.
Direction on the table
Proposed: enforcement lives in the same Integral validation layer above VF — the no-conversion rule sits with the forbidden-action discipline, not in VF itself.
Worth weighing: do we encode node identity inside VF's Agent, or keep our own node_id + federation_hash and map to VF on export?

// The balance sheet

What adoption buys, and what it costs

Buys us
+ A mature, twenty-year accounting vocabulary — and off-the-shelf semantics for inventory, recipes, and event-sourced ledgers that already match our append-only rule.
Buys us
+ Interoperability with the wider commons-economy world already on VF — we wouldn't reinvent resource-flow bookkeeping, and we'd speak the same language as adjacent projects.
Costs us
− It covers three of five systems only. CDS and FRS stay Integral-native and must be specified separately.
Costs us
− Its neutrality means we must adopt a deliberately constrained profile — or risk diluting the post-monetary core.
The recommendation: adopt ValueFlows as a constrained profile for OAD, COS, and ITC — and keep CDS and FRS as Integral-native layers that read from the VF substrate rather than live inside it. Apply the Dev Guide's test at every step: would it still be recognizably Integral? With the constraints in place, the answer stays yes.
// What the group needs to decide

Five questions for deliberation

This is a cross-system architectural choice — a Tier-2 decision, settled by documented consensus, not a single call. None of these are decided. Deciding them together is the work.

Q1Profile, not wholesale adoption?+
The question
Do we adopt VF as a constrained profile for OAD/COS/ITC rather than wholesale — and who drafts the constraint list (forbidden actions, non-mediumOfExchange resources)?
Q2Target VF directly, or map on export?+
The question
Phase 1's schema exercise produces our canonical schemas. Do those target VF classes directly, or do we keep Integral-native schemas with a VF export mapping? DEV GUIDE §5.1
Q3Adopt VF's recipe-vs-design split?+
The question
Do we follow VF in separating the executable Recipe from the design artefact (a resource with lineage), restructuring CertifiedDesign accordingly?
Q4How is credit issuance modelled?+
The question
Confirm the issuance pattern: a reciprocal event paired to the work event — and whether an Agreement / Claim mediates it. The mapping author flags this as still open. VALUEFLOWS
Q5Where does "recognition without conversion" live?+
The question
Does the federation no-conversion rule live in the VF profile, or in an Integral validation layer above it? WHITE PAPER §10
